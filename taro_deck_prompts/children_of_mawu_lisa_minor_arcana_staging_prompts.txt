SHARED CHASSIS
----------------

Act as a cinematographer and visual layout artist working on a tarot deck called Children of Mawu-Lisa, rooted in Ewe cosmology. Generate staging, blocking, and composition ideas as cinematic previsualization — tabletop maquette photography using wooden artist's puppets, cloth scraps, paper cutouts, and practical set lighting. The goal is NOT finished illustration. The goal is NOT a character render. The goal is symbolic readability, ceremonial blocking, graphic composition hierarchy, and theatrical emotional staging captured as visibly handmade puppet photography on a small physical stage.

PREVIZ RENDER REQUIREMENT (applies to every card):
The image should read as a photograph of a small tabletop set. Puppets are posable wooden artist's dolls with visible ball joints at shoulders, elbows, hips, and knees, AND articulated wooden hand mannequins attached at the wrists with individual posable wooden fingers. Hands should read as separate jointed wooden pieces, not the mitten-shaped hands of standard artist's dolls. Puppet heads are sculpted wooden forms with defined jawlines and chins — closer to a lay figure or stop-motion armature head than the smooth egg-shaped head of a standard artist's doll. Heads have basic sculpted facial geometry — a defined jawline and chin, a nose ridge with visible nostrils suggested, and shallow indented eye sockets where the eyes would sit — but no painted features (no irises, no eyebrows, no lips). Any face markings called for in a specific card (moon dots, sun motifs, ancestral lines, pock-motifs) are painted directly onto the bare wooden surface over this sculpted geometry. Puppets are dressed in cloth scraps and paper costume elements. Backdrops are painted paper or fabric. Props are obviously handmade — cardboard, foil, dowel rods, found objects. Lighting is practical lamps with colored gels. The viewer should be able to tell it is a maquette, not an illustration.

STAGING APPROACH:
Treat every scene as a ceremonial tableau / miniature stage play / sacred poster composition / theatrical symbolic freeze-frame — built as a physical tabletop diorama.

PRIORITIES:
Readable silhouettes, emotional clarity, strong focal hierarchy, reusable staging setups, minimal clutter, symbolic object placement, iconic tarot framing, simplified environmental design.

AVOID:
Realism-first rendering, character illustration, anime or manga character renders, 3D character models, stylized human figures, smooth CGI skin, finished illustration, busy backgrounds, decorative clutter, dynamic perspective distortion unless narratively necessary.

REUSABLE MAQUETTE RIGS (build once, reuse across grouped cards):
- RIG A — Throne / Icon Rig: seated wooden puppet on cardboard throne block, frontal symmetrical staging, top-down or side-key lighting, hanging halo cutout overhead. Prototypes High Priestess, Empress, Emperor (and Queen of Swords/Pentacles when you build them).
- RIG B — Duality / Mirror Rig: two wooden puppets facing each other on centerline, split-gel lighting (cool/warm), reflective acrylic floor or mirror panel between them. Prototypes The Lovers (and Two of Cups).
- RIG C — Cosmic Emblem Rig: no figure. Circular paper cutout or coiled rope on dark cloth, overhead light rig, radial composition. Prototypes Wheel of Fortune (and Card Back).
- RIG D — Transformation / Power Rig: wooden puppet with raffia/cloth silhouette extensions, low side key with red or amber gel, textured paper ground, optional torn-paper smoke and flashlight lightning streaks. Prototypes Death, Tower, Sun, Knight of Wands.

THUMBNAIL WORKFLOW RECOMMENDATION:
Before any AI generation or final render, produce 3 value thumbnails per card (2x3 inch, grayscale, 5-minute limit each) testing silhouette clarity at deck-size (approximately 2.75 x 4.75 in). If the silhouette doesn't read at thumbnail scale, the staging fails — revise blocking before adding any detail.

MINOR ARCANA SPECIFIC NOTES — PASTE SECOND

REGISTER SHIFT — IMPORTANT:
Minor Arcana cards are NOT deity portraits. The Majors depict named cosmological figures (Mawu, Lisa, Nana Buluku, Sakpata, Sogbo) with monumental, iconic gravity. The Minors depict spirits, attendants, or human stand-ins within each Tro's elemental domain. They are:
- More accessible than Majors — closer to human scale, less monumental
- More emotionally expressive — wider range of gestures and gazes
- More environmentally grounded — placed inside the Tro's element (water, fire, sky, earth) rather than against neutral symbolic voids
- Still puppet-mode previz — same wooden puppets, same articulated hands, same sculpted heads with no painted features

Court cards (Knight of Wands, Queen of Swords, Queen of Pentacles) sit between these poles: they are named figures within their Tro's domain (warriors, queens) but not the deities themselves. Render them as elevated but not monumental — slightly grander than pip figures, clearly less imposing than Majors.

SUIT PALETTES (apply per suit throughout):
- Cups (Agbe — Water): deep blues, aqua, silver, white-clay accents. Backgrounds incorporate water in some form — rippled surfaces, riverbanks, reflections.
- Wands (Sogbo — Fire): deep reds, ochres, dark purples, gold accents, lightning whites. Backgrounds incorporate storm sky, smoke, or fire glow.
- Swords (Djo — Wind): cool grays, sky blues, soft whites, silver accents. Backgrounds incorporate clouds, wind streaks, open sky.
- Pentacles (Sakpata / Age — Earth): rich browns, deep greens, gold, ochre, warm earth tones. Backgrounds incorporate fertile ground, growing plants, garden archways.

NEW RIG ADDED FOR MINORS:
- RIG E — Elemental Hand Rig: no full figure. A single oversized wooden puppet hand (articulated, with individual fingers visible) emerges from an elemental field — water surface, storm clouds, wind clouds, or fertile earth. The hand holds or offers the suit object (calabash, sprouting wand, blade, golden disc). Background is the elemental field itself, treated as a layered cutout or textured backdrop. Prototypes all four Aces (Cups, Wands, Swords, Pentacles). Reuse logic: same isolated articulated hand prop, redressed with different elemental backdrops and different suit objects across the four Aces. Build once, redress four times.

EXISTING RIGS USED IN THE MINORS:
- RIG A (Throne / Icon): Queen of Swords, Queen of Pentacles
- RIG B (Duality / Mirror): Two of Cups (variant — kneeling figures across water instead of mirrored standing figures)
- RIG D (Transformation / Power, kinetic variant): Knight of Wands

FLOORPLAN USAGE FOR MINORS:
Floorplans are included only for the two cards with multi-figure or multi-element spatial complexity:
- Two of Cups (two figures, river between, calabashes exchanging mid-frame)
- Knight of Wands (rider, mount, raised staff, trailing banner, smoke)

Aces and Queens skip floorplans because their compositions are centered, single-element, and unambiguous from prose.


CARD 1 — ACE OF CUPS
Rig: E — Elemental Hand
Suit: Cups (Agbe — Water)

SCENE PURPOSE:
New emotional opening. Love and intuition flowing in. A sacred offering from the water itself.

ENVIRONMENT:
Rippled water in deep teal, fading to lighter blue at the top of frame. The water surface IS the ground plane. No horizon, no architecture — the whole environment is the water Tro's domain rising up.

CAMERA:
Straight-on medium shot, slightly low. Camera at water-surface level looking very slightly up at the emerging hand. Stable, no tilt.

CHARACTER ACTION:
A single oversized wooden puppet hand (articulated, with individual posable fingers) emerges from the water surface, palm up, fingers cupped, holding a calabash overflowing with water.

FOCAL POINT:
The overflowing calabash on the wooden hand. Water spilling over the rim catches the eye and leads it downward into the rippled surface.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Centered vertical composition. Hand and forearm rise from lower-third of frame, calabash at center-frame, water overflow trailing downward back into the surface. Small details (water lily, fish, descending bird) scatter at edges without crowding the center.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Calabash centered on the wooden palm. Water lily floats on the surface to the left of the hand. Small fish leaps to the right of the hand, mid-arc. Kingfisher or dove descends from upper frame holding a paper leaf — small, off-center upper-left or upper-right.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Strong central vertical anchored by the hand and calabash. Soft circular motion implied by the water ripples radiating outward. Negative space at top of frame for the descending bird.

LIGHTING:
Cool reflective lighting. Soft blue-white key from upper-front, with secondary reflections bouncing off the water surface back onto the underside of the wooden hand. No warm tones.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:
- Overflowing calabash on the wooden hand
- Small water lily on the surface
- Small fish leaping
- Kingfisher or dove descending with a paper leaf in beak

MOOD / INFLUENCE:
Sacred tenderness. Abundant freshness. The water Tro offering itself.

READABILITY GOAL:
Audience must feel sacred overflow. Silhouette reads at thumbnail as: single vertical hand with rounded calabash at top, water rippling at base.

PERSPECTIVE GRID:
1-point perspective, vanishing point centered behind the hand at calabash height. Water surface tilts slightly toward camera for visibility.

CHARACTER ORIENTATION:
Hand emerges straight up from the water, palm rotated toward camera. Wooden fingers visible and individually articulated, cradling the calabash from below.

PHYSICAL STAGING APPROACH (maquette):
- Single articulated wooden hand mannequin (forearm and hand only), mounted upright through a hole cut in a sheet of textured blue paper or thin acrylic representing the water surface
- Small clay or wooden bowl as calabash prop, with real water or clear acrylic gel for overflow effect
- Layered acetate ripples placed concentrically around the hand on the water surface
- Paper water lily, small toy fish on monofilament, paper bird prop suspended above
- Reflective foil scraps under the water sheet to catch downward light

LAYERED CUTOUT IDEAS:
Four planes — (1) deep teal-to-light-blue gradient backdrop, (2) bird descending from upper frame, (3) emerging wooden hand with calabash, (4) water surface plane with lily, fish, and ripples in foreground.

ACRYLIC GRID SUGGESTION:
Centered vertical axis. Calabash on upper-third horizontal. Water surface on lower-third horizontal. Bird positioned on upper-third intersection (left or right, not center).

COLORED GEL LIGHTING:
Full CTB blue gel on upper-front key. Optional pale cyan accent from below the water surface to backlight ripples. No amber, no warm tones.

AI VISUALIZATION GUIDANCE:
Tabletop maquette photography. Visibly handmade puppet scene on a small physical stage. A single oversized articulated wooden hand mannequin (forearm and hand, with visible joints and individual posable wooden fingers) emerges vertically from a rippled water surface made of textured blue paper and layered acetate. The wooden palm holds a small clay calabash overflowing with real water spilling down the wrist. A paper water lily floats on the surface to one side; a small toy fish leaps from the water on the other side; a small paper kingfisher or dove with a paper leaf in its beak descends from the upper frame on monofilament. Deep teal painted-paper backdrop fading to lighter blue at top. Cool blue lighting from upper-front lamp with CTB gel. Centered vertical composition, low frontal medium shot. Render as set photography, not as character illustration.

NEGATIVE PROMPTS:
anime, manga character render, 3D character model, stylized human figure, smooth CGI skin, illustrated character, finished illustration, mitten hands, fused fingers, blob hands, fingerless puppet hands, painted anime face, painted character features, drawn-on eyes, drawn-on eyebrows, faceless puppet head, realistic water effects, photographic realism, busy underwater scene, full figure visible.

Eventual finished card will translate this staging into a Dahomey appliqué illustration with flat symbolic color fields, bold silhouettes, selective Krobo beadwork on the wrist if visible, and stylized water rendering with appliqué wave shapes. Do not render that style at the previz stage.


CARD 2 — TWO OF CUPS
Rig: B — Duality / Mirror (variant)
Suit: Cups (Agbe — Water)

SCENE PURPOSE:
Partnership, union, mutual attraction. Two becoming one through an exchange across water. Intimate, balanced, gentle.

ENVIRONMENT:
Calm river at golden hour. The narrow river runs horizontally across the lower third of the frame. Gentle blues with warm gold reflections on the water surface. River banks rise on either side as low painted-paper berms.

CAMERA:
Wide symmetrical frontal shot. Eye level with the kneeling puppets. Slight downward tilt to show the river surface and the meeting point of hands.

CHARACTER ACTION:
Two wooden puppets kneeling on opposite banks of a narrow river, leaning forward at the waist to exchange small calabash props. Their wooden hands meet above the center of the water.

FOCAL POINT:
The meeting point — wooden hands and calabashes touching above the water at exact center frame. Reflections of the hands shimmer below in the water surface.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Mirror composition. Puppet 1 screen-left, kneeling on the left riverbank, body angled inward. Puppet 2 screen-right, mirrored. River centerline runs left-to-right across lower-third. Hands and calabashes meet at center-frame at the horizontal midline.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Calabashes meet at exact frame center, held at chest height of the kneeling puppets. Small toy fish circling in the river just below the meeting point. Matching beadwork visible at the puppets' wrists or necks. Riverbanks slope downward toward the river at a gentle angle.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Bilateral symmetry. Cool/warm clothing echoes Mawu-Lisa duality at miniature scale — Puppet 1 in cooler cloth scraps (silver-blue), Puppet 2 in warmer cloth scraps (gold-ochre). The river reflection doubles the meeting point — the hands and their shimmering reflections together form a near-circle at center.

LIGHTING:
Soft warm key from camera-right at low angle (golden-hour sunlight). Cooler ambient fill on the water and the screen-left puppet. Reflections on the water catch both warm and cool tones.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:
- Two calabash props meeting at frame center
- Small toy fish circling in the river
- Matching paper or bead string accents on both puppets
- Cool/warm cloth-scrap garments echoing duality

MOOD / INFLUENCE:
Intimate, balanced, gentle. Quiet partnership. Not romantic embrace — sacred exchange.

READABILITY GOAL:
Audience must feel mutual offering. Silhouette reads at thumbnail as: two kneeling figures leaning in over a horizontal water band, meeting at center.

PERSPECTIVE GRID:
1-point perspective, low horizon at the river surface. Vanishing point centered behind the meeting point of the calabashes.

CHARACTER ORIENTATION:
Both puppets in 3/4 view, angled inward toward the centerline. Wooden torsos leaning forward from kneeling positions. Wooden heads turned to face the meeting point — not the camera. Their gazes lock on the calabashes, not on each other and not on the viewer.

PHYSICAL STAGING APPROACH (maquette):
- Two wooden artist's puppets in kneeling poses on either side of a narrow water trough or sheet of mirrored acrylic painted blue
- Low painted-paper berms on the outsides of each puppet representing riverbanks
- Two small clay calabash props meeting at the geometric center of the frame
- Small toy fish on monofilament in the water trough, circling under the meeting point
- Warm lamp with CTO gel camera-right at low angle for golden-hour key
- Cool blue ambient fill from above-camera-left

CARD 3 — ACE OF WANDS
Rig: E — Elemental Hand
Suit: Wands (Sogbo — Fire)

Spark of inspiration. Creative ignition. The beginning of passion erupting from Sogbo's storm sky.

A single oversized wooden puppet hand (articulated, with individual fingers) thrusts upward from a cluster of storm clouds, gripping a wooden staff that sprouts green paper leaves at the top. Lightning crackles around the staff's base.

The sprouting top of the staff where leaves emerge. Lightning streaks radiate outward from the staff's base, leading the eye back down to the wooden hand's grip.

Diagonal energy composition. Wooden hand and staff form a strong diagonal from lower-frame to upper-frame, slightly off-vertical. Storm clouds cluster at lower-third of frame around the wrist. Lightning streaks scatter asymmetrically.

Staff held vertically (or near-vertical with slight tilt) in the wooden hand's fist. Green paper leaves clustered at the staff's top, partially silhouetted against the sky. Small orange paper flame at the staff's base where it meets the wooden grip. Lightning cutouts radiate from the wrist area.

LIGHTING:

CARD 4 — KNIGHT OF WANDS
Rig: D — Transformation / Power (kinetic variant)
Suit: Wands (Sogbo — Fire)

SCENE PURPOSE:
Action, adventure, passionate movement. Charging ahead. The most action-driven Minor — a warrior in motion through fire.

ENVIRONMENT:
Warm sunset reds and oranges painted-paper backdrop with smoke streaks rendered as torn paper. Suggested ground plane below, but the focus is the warrior in motion through hot air.

CAMERA:
Medium shot, slight low angle for heroic framing. Camera positioned screen-right, capturing the charge moving from right-to-left across the frame (or vice versa — pick one and commit). Slight Dutch tilt (5-10 degrees) to amplify motion.

CHARACTER ACTION:
A wooden puppet warrior posed on top of a toy mount (model horse or ram), mid-gallop. The puppet leans forward at the waist, one wooden arm raised high gripping a staff topped with a small paper flame. The other wooden hand grips the mount's neck or rein. A long cloth-scrap banner trails behind.

FOCAL POINT:
