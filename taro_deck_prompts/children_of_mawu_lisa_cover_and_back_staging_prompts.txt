COVER & BACK SPECIFIC NOTES — PASTE SECOND

DESIGN INTENT:
The cover and back must signal "this is the whole deck" — the foundational cosmological logic of Children of Mawu-Lisa, compressed into a single image. Unlike individual cards, the cover/back is not narrating a meaning — it is announcing the deck itself.

The cover designs explore the theme of duality. Mawu-Lisa is shown as both male and female, yet separate or unified depending on the composition. One side is linked to the sun, the other to the moon. The serpent Aido-Hwedo (who wraps around the earth) appears in some of the designs.

AGENT-CHOICE FRAMING:
Four cover options are presented as a slate for the agent to choose from. They are not progressive iterations — each represents a different statement about the deck:
- Cover Option A (Sketch 1): One being split anatomically — duality made flesh, surrounded by the serpent's protective embrace
- Cover Option B (Sketch 2): Mawu and Lisa standing back-to-back, spines touching, each facing their own elemental domain — duality as intimacy through opposition
- Cover Option C (Sketch 3): One unified figure expressing duality through paint and adornment — duality embodied
- Cover Option D (Sketch 4): The serpent and the earth — duality as cosmological framework, no figures

Two back options are presented:
- Back Option A (Sketch 5): Sun-Moon Half Emblem — duality as a single divided emblem, deep indigo field
- Back Option B (Sketch 6): Aido-Hwedo Ouroboros — serpent biting tail around the earth, pale sky blue field

REGISTER:
The cover is the most monumental composition in the deck — closer in register to the Major Arcana (deity portraits) than to the Minor Arcana (everyday spirits). The puppets here represent Mawu and Lisa themselves, not stand-ins. Render with the dignity of the deities they portray.

The back is purely emblematic — no figures, no narrative. The puppets are absent; the composition is pure symbol.

CONCERN FLAG FOR COVER OPTION A:
Cover Option A (Sketch 1) presents a more contemplative, devotional register than the other three cover options. Its strength is its literal embodiment of duality through split anatomy — a single body, half-Mawu, half-Lisa, embraced by the cosmic serpent. Its risk is that the seated/standing meditative pose may read quieter than the agent expects from a deck cover. This option is included for its conceptual depth; the agent should weigh whether its register communicates "cover" effectively against the more declarative compositions of Options B, C, and D.

BORDER FOR ALL SIX COMPOSITIONS:
Every cover and back composition uses the same physical border element built into the maquette. Append the BORDER PHYSICAL SPEC paragraph from the BORDER SPECIFICATION document to the AI VISUALIZATION GUIDANCE of each composition.


COMPOSITION 1 — COVER OPTION A (Split-Body Mawu-Lisa with Serpent Embrace)
Source: Sketch 1
Rig: A — Throne / Icon (variant: standing, not seated)

SCENE PURPOSE:
Mawu-Lisa as one being in two forms — duality made flesh through anatomical split, embraced and protected by the cosmic serpent Aido-Hwedo. The deck's foundational thesis: divinity is rarely singular.

ENVIRONMENT:
Vertical gradient backdrop, painted-paper. Deep indigo on the left half, transitioning to warm ochre/red on the right half along a clean centerline. The seam where the two halves meet runs through the exact center of the frame, aligning with the figure's anatomical split.

CAMERA:
Straight-on frontal medium shot. Eye level with the figure's chest. No tilt. Symmetrical, ceremonial framing.

CHARACTER ACTION:
A single wooden puppet standing frontally, with anatomical split down the centerline. The puppet's body is divided vertically: left half is Mawu (woman), right half is Lisa (man). The wooden head shows both halves meeting — long-hair styling on the Mawu side, shorter-hair styling on the Lisa side. Both wooden hands cupped together at waist level cradling a small calabash prop.

FOCAL POINT:
The combined sun-moon hybrid halo behind the figure's head (moon on left, sun on right), then the calabash held at the center.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Centered vertical composition. Figure occupies the central column, taking up the lower two-thirds of frame. Sun-moon halo behind the head extends into the upper-third. Serpent (Aido-Hwedo) curves in an S-shape — C-curve over the upper-left around the figure's left shoulder, reverse-C below at the figure's right side — forming an embracing serpentine frame around the figure.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Calabash centered on the body's centerline at waist level, cupped between both wooden hands. Sun-moon halo directly behind the head, centered, with moon on screen-left half and sun on screen-right half. Aido-Hwedo serpent in S-curve: serpent head visible at upper-screen-left coming over the figure's left shoulder; body curves down and around through the lower-screen-right area; tail visible at lower-screen-right.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Strict bilateral split. Every element on the left half has its inverse on the right half:
- Left half: indigo backdrop, Mawu's woman face/hair, moon halo half, cool silver/white/pale blue cloth scraps
- Right half: ochre backdrop, Lisa's man face/hair, sun halo half, warm gold/red/ochre cloth scraps
The serpent's S-curve is the only asymmetric element, providing graceful contrast against the strict centerline split.

LIGHTING:
Split warm/cool dual lighting, mirroring the Lovers card logic but on a single figure. Cool blue key from screen-left lights Mawu's side of the puppet. Warm amber key from screen-right lights Lisa's side. Lights cross at the centerline — the meeting point is where the split happens. Soft neutral light catches the calabash at center.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:
- Split-body wooden puppet (Mawu left / Lisa right)
- Calabash prop cupped at waist level
- Sun-moon hybrid halo behind head (moon left half, sun right half)
- Aido-Hwedo serpent in S-curve embrace around the figure
- Cool/warm split cloth scraps as garment

MOOD / INFLUENCE:
Devotional, contemplative, sacred. The deck's cosmological core stated as a single image.

READABILITY GOAL:
The viewer must read this as "two principles, one being." Silhouette at thumbnail: vertical centered figure with split halo at top, serpent curving around it.

PERSPECTIVE GRID:
1-point perspective with vanishing point dead-centered behind the figure's head. Horizon centered.

CHARACTER ORIENTATION:
Front view. Wooden torso facing camera. Wooden head straight, divided down the centerline. Wooden hands cupped together at waist level on centerline.

PHYSICAL STAGING APPROACH (maquette):
- Wooden artist's puppet posed standing, with cloth scraps applied to split the body vertically: cool silver/white/pale blue on the wooden left side, warm gold/red/ochre on the wooden right side
- The wooden head can be subtly painted on each half — cool wash on the left, warm wash on the right — without painting facial features
- Hair styling: small cloth or paper extensions for "long hair" on the left (Mawu), shorter or absent on the right (Lisa)
- Small clay calabash prop cupped between articulated wooden hands at waist
- Sun-moon hybrid halo cutout (white foamcore for moon half, foil-laminated ochre paper for sun half) suspended behind the head
- Coiled rope or thick painted cord serpent prop arranged in an S-curve around the figure, secured with monofilament or museum putty
- Two-toned painted-paper backdrop split indigo/ochre at the vertical centerline
- Split-gel lighting: blue gel screen-left, amber gel screen-right

LAYERED CUTOUT IDEAS:
Five planes — (1) indigo/ochre split backdrop, (2) sun-moon halo behind head, (3) split-body wooden puppet with calabash, (4) serpent in S-curve embrace, (5) physical border frame (outermost plane).

ACRYLIC GRID SUGGESTION:
Strict centerline vertical axis. Calabash on horizontal midline. Halo on upper-third horizontal. Serpent head on upper-third intersection (screen-left); serpent tail on lower-third intersection (screen-right).

COLORED GEL LIGHTING:
CTB blue gel screen-left key. CTO amber gel screen-right key. Both at equal intensity. Optional faint lavender accent on the calabash at center to mark the meeting point as sacred.

A single wooden artist's puppet with visible ball joints, articulated wooden hand mannequins attached at the wrists with individual posable fingers, and a sculpted wooden head with a defined jawline, chin, nose ridge, and shallow indented eye sockets (lay-figure or stop-motion armature style; sculpted facial geometry only, with no painted features such as irises, eyebrows, or lips), posed standing frontally. COMPOSITION 2 — COVER OPTION B (Back-to-Back Mawu and Lisa, Mawu Almost Hidden)
Source: Sketch 2
Rig: B — Duality / Mirror (back-to-back variant, with rear figure obscured)

SCENE PURPOSE:
Mawu and Lisa as two principles sharing one center. Their spines touch — they are inseparable — but each faces their own elemental domain. Lisa stands forward and dominates the composition; Mawu stands almost entirely hidden behind him, her presence signaled only by her profile head turned to screen-left, her moon halo, and a thin sliver of her body emerging at screen-left. Duality as intimacy through opposition, with the moon-bound principle held within the sun-bound principle's silhouette.

ENVIRONMENT:
Painted-paper backdrop with a vertical split: warm ochre on screen-right (Lisa's side, facing camera) transitioning to deep indigo on screen-left (Mawu's side, where her halo and profile peek out from behind Lisa). Rolling hill or cloud shapes at the lower frame, made from torn paper.

CAMERA:
Straight-on frontal medium shot. Eye level with Lisa's chest. No tilt. Centered framing on Lisa, with Mawu's profile and moon halo peeking out at screen-left.

CHARACTER ACTION:
Two wooden puppets. Lisa stands in front, facing camera squarely, his feet together. He holds a small coiled Aido-Hwedo serpent prop in both wooden hands at chest level. Mawu stands directly behind Lisa with her wooden spine touching his, almost fully obscured by Lisa's silhouette from the camera's POV. Her wooden body is rotated 90 degrees from Lisa's — her back faces his back. She is posed in a casual contrapposto stance (one wooden hand on her screen-facing hip, the other wooden arm relaxed at her side, weight settled into one leg with the other knee slightly relaxed, feet naturally apart) — but the camera sees almost none of this because Lisa's silhouette covers her body. What IS visible to the camera: her wooden head turned in profile facing screen-left, peeking out past Lisa's screen-left shoulder so the camera catches a side view of her face; and a thin sliver of her hip or shoulder line emerging at screen-left to confirm her physical presence behind Lisa.

FOCAL POINT:
The held serpent at Lisa's chest level — small enough to be a single emblem, large enough to read as Aido-Hwedo. Secondary focal point: Mawu's profile face and moon halo visible at screen-left past Lisa's shoulder.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Lisa centered, frontal, dominates the composition — his silhouette fills the central column from feet to head and almost fully obscures Mawu behind him. Mawu is positioned directly behind Lisa with her spine touching his; her body is almost completely hidden by Lisa from the camera POV. What's visible of Mawu: her profile head peeking out past Lisa's screen-left shoulder, and a thin sliver of her hip or shoulder line at screen-left confirming her presence. Sun halo behind Lisa's head, centered on him. Moon halo behind Mawu's head, positioned at screen-left, visible past Lisa's shoulder line. Rolling hill silhouettes at lower-third frame.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Coiled serpent prop held by Lisa at chest level on his body centerline. Sun halo cutout directly behind Lisa's wooden head, centered on Lisa's body. Moon halo cutout behind Mawu's wooden head at screen-left, sized similarly to the sun halo, visible past Lisa's shoulder line. Mawu's wooden hands are posed (one on hip, one at side) but mostly hidden by Lisa's body — only a thin sliver of her hip or shoulder emerges at screen-left. Rolling hill shapes spread across lower-third frame.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Asymmetric balance through back-to-back staging with Mawu almost fully obscured. Lisa is the dominant centered silhouette and visually carries the composition; Mawu's profile head and moon halo at screen-left, plus a thin sliver of body emerging behind Lisa, are the only confirmations of her presence. The two figures share one centerline at the spine — that touch is invisible to the viewer but it's what holds the composition together. Cool/warm cloth scraps echo Mawu-Lisa duality (Mawu in cool tones, Lisa in warm tones), but only Mawu's halo and small visible portions read cool to the camera. They cannot turn to face each other because their backs are joined — they ARE together rather than choosing to be together. The composition asks the viewer to read Mawu's presence partly through implication rather than full visibility.

LIGHTING:
Split warm/cool dual lighting. Warm amber key from camera-right at low angle lights Lisa from the front. Cool blue key from screen-left lights Mawu's profile head and the visible sliver of her body at screen-left. The two lights cross behind the figures (where the spines touch) and don't compete on either figure's face.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:
- Small coiled Aido-Hwedo serpent prop held by Lisa at chest
- Sun halo cutout behind Lisa's head (screen-center)
- Moon halo cutout behind Mawu's head (screen-left, visible past Lisa's shoulder)
- Cool cloth scraps for Mawu (mostly hidden behind Lisa), warm cloth scraps for Lisa
- Rolling hill silhouettes at lower frame

MOOD / INFLUENCE:
Intimate, inseparable, two who travel in opposite directions while sharing one body. The most profound expression of the deck's duality — they cannot turn to face each other because their backs are joined, and Mawu is mostly hidden because she travels within Lisa's silhouette. Her presence is felt more than seen.

READABILITY GOAL:
The viewer must feel two principles unified at the spine — Lisa dominant and forward-facing, Mawu almost hidden behind him with only her profile and moon halo signaling her presence. Silhouette at thumbnail: one tall figure centered with a profile head and crescent halo peeking out at screen-left, two halos at upper frame (sun centered, moon offset).

PERSPECTIVE GRID:
1-point perspective, low horizon at the hill line. Vanishing point centered behind Lisa at chest level.

CHARACTER ORIENTATION:
Lisa: front view, wooden torso square to camera, feet together. Mawu: rear/profile orientation behind Lisa, wooden torso rotated 90 degrees from Lisa's (her back to his back), wooden head in profile facing screen-left, feet slightly apart in contrapposto. Most of Mawu's body is hidden behind Lisa from camera POV; only her profile head and a sliver of her side emerge at screen-left.

COMPOSITION 3 — COVER OPTION C (Single Figure with Split Markings)
Source: Sketch 3
Rig: A — Throne / Icon (variant: standing, not seated)

SCENE PURPOSE:
One figure, one body, dual nature — duality expressed through paint and adornment rather than anatomy. The most subtle of the cover options. Says: divinity in two natures is not always two beings.

ENVIRONMENT:
Painted-paper backdrop, vertical split: deep indigo on the left half transitioning to warm ochre on the right half. Rolling hill silhouettes at lower frame, same treatment as Cover Option B.

CAMERA:
Straight-on frontal medium-full shot. Eye level. The figure is taller and more elongated in framing than in the other cover options — closer to a full-length portrait.

CHARACTER ACTION:
A single wooden puppet standing frontally, tall and elongated in pose. Wooden hands resting at the puppet's sides (not holding anything). Wooden head straight, facing camera. The body is NOT anatomically split (unlike Cover Option A) — it is a unified single body, but it expresses duality through paint and adornment.

FOCAL POINT:
The wooden head with its split face markings, then the combined sun-moon halo behind the head.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Centered vertical composition. The single figure occupies the central column, taking up the full height of the inner frame from feet to halo. Backdrop divides vertically behind the figure. Hill silhouettes at lower frame.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Combined sun-moon halo directly behind the wooden head, centered. The halo is rendered as a single radial form with paper sun rays on the screen-right half and a white foamcore crescent moon on the screen-left half, meeting at the centerline. Wooden hands resting at the puppet's sides.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Single tall vertical silhouette. The duality is concentrated in the FACE PAINT and the ADORNMENT, not in the body shape:
- Face markings: White clay moon-phase dots painted on the screen-left half of the wooden face; ochre sun motifs painted on the screen-right half
- Asymmetric headpiece: A cloth wrap or paper crown with moon emblem on the screen-left side and sun emblem on the screen-right side
- Different beadwork on each wooden wrist: cool silver/white beads on the screen-left wrist, warm gold/ochre beads on the screen-right wrist
- Garments: Cloth scraps split cool/warm down the body centerline, joining at the chest seam — the cool side on screen-left, warm side on screen-right

LIGHTING:
Even split warm/cool dual lighting, same logic as Cover Option A but more subtle. Cool blue key from screen-left lights the cool side of the figure. Warm amber key from screen-right lights the warm side. Less dramatic crossing than Option A — more like ambient bi-directional light than two distinct beams.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:
- Combined sun-moon halo (sun rays right, crescent moon left)
- Split face markings (moon dots left, sun motifs right) painted on the wooden head
- Asymmetric headpiece with moon and sun emblems
- Split-color beadwork at each wrist
- Cool/warm split cloth-scrap garments

MOOD / INFLUENCE:
Unified, ceremonial, rooted in West African body-marking tradition. Less declarative than Option A's anatomical split, more layered through symbol.

READABILITY GOAL:
The viewer must read this as "one being with two natures." Silhouette at thumbnail: single tall figure with combined halo and clearly split face/body coloring.

PERSPECTIVE GRID:
1-point perspective, vanishing point centered behind the figure. Horizon centered or slightly low.

CHARACTER ORIENTATION:
Front view. Wooden torso square to camera. Wooden head straight. Wooden hands at sides. Total bilateral symmetry except for the split coloring/ornament.

PHYSICAL STAGING APPROACH (maquette):
- Single wooden artist's puppet in standing pose
- The wooden head: paint white clay moon-phase dots on the screen-left half of the face; paint small ochre sun motifs on the screen-right half (mark directly on the bare wooden surface — these are the card-specific markings allowed by the chassis)
- Cloth scraps wrapped around the body: cool silver/white/pale blue on the puppet's screen-left half (vertical seam down the centerline), warm gold/red/ochre on the screen-right half
- Headpiece: a cloth wrap with a small foamcore crescent moon emblem on the screen-left and a small foil-laminated paper sun emblem on the screen-right
- Bead strings at each wooden wrist: cool-toned beads on the screen-left wrist, warm-toned beads on the screen-right wrist
- Combined halo: half-crescent-moon cutout (screen-left) and half-sun-rays cutout (screen-right) joined at the centerline, suspended behind the wooden head
- Painted-paper backdrop split indigo (screen-left) and ochre (screen-right)
- Cool blue CTB lamp screen-left, warm amber CTO lamp screen-right, at equal intensity

LAYERED CUTOUT IDEAS:
Five planes — (1) indigo/ochre split backdrop, (2) combined sun-moon halo, (3) single wooden puppet with split markings and split garments, (4) rolling hill silhouettes foreground, (5) physical border frame (outermost).

ACRYLIC GRID SUGGESTION:
