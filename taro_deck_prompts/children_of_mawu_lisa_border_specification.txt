CHILDREN OF MAWU-LISA — BORDER SPECIFICATION
Reusable Spec for All Card Types
PUPPET-MODE PREVIZ EDITION


PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT

This is a shared specification for the border treatment used across every card in the Children of Mawu-Lisa deck — Major Arcana, Minor Arcana, Cover, and Back. The border is a real physical element in the puppet maquette stage, not a digital post-production overlay. It should be built, lit, and photographed as part of the previz scene.

Reference this document any time you generate a card. Append the BORDER PHYSICAL SPEC paragraph (below) to the AI VISUALIZATION GUIDANCE section of any card prompt to ensure the border renders consistently.


BORDER DESIGN SUMMARY

Universal across the deck:
- Clean rectangular black line frame around every card
- Modest corner ornaments at all four corners, drawn in a style inspired by Dahomey appliqué and Adinkra symbols (NOT European Art Nouveau botanicals)
- Corner ornaments rendered in gold or ochre
- Main frame line rendered in black
- Crisp clean paper background (not aged, not weathered)
- Same black line work universal across all 18 cards plus cover and back

Card-specific text within the border:
- Major Arcana: Roman numeral at top center inside the frame, card name in a bottom banner ("THE HIGH PRIESTESS")
- Minor Arcana: Card name only in a bottom banner ("ACE OF CUPS"), no top numeral
- Cover: Title "CHILDREN OF MAWU-LISA" — placement varies by cover option, see Cover & Back Staging Brief
- Back: No text


CORNER ORNAMENT MOTIF OPTIONS

The corner ornaments should be drawn in a style that reads as West African cosmological — small, graphic, NOT botanical or floral. Pick one of these motif families and use it consistently across the deck (do not mix multiple motif styles within the same deck):

Option 1 — Coiled Serpent Curl:
A small spiral or coil reading as a miniature Aido-Hwedo. Ties every card to the deck's serpentine cosmological core. The most narrative motif.

Option 2 — Stylized Cowrie:
A small cowrie shape (small oval with a slit down the center). Ties to the deck's beadwork tradition and to Sakpata / earth associations. The most ancestral motif.

Option 3 — Sun-Moon Half Emblem:
A tiny circle split half-and-half — half sun rays, half moon crescent. Ties to the deck's Mawu-Lisa duality at miniature scale. The most cosmologically-pure motif.

Option 4 — Adinkra-Adjacent Geometric Symbol:
A small geometric motif inspired by but not directly copying Adinkra symbols. Could be a curl, a triangle nest, or a stylized star. The most graphic / abstract motif.

RECOMMENDED: Option 1 (Coiled Serpent Curl) for narrative continuity with the deck's Aido-Hwedo back design. The corner curls echo the back's full serpent at miniature scale, creating visual repetition across the deck that reinforces the cosmological logic.

The corner ornament should be:
- Modest in size — occupying roughly 1/15th to 1/12th the height of the card edge
- Drawn in gold or ochre line work, NOT filled solid
- Identical at all four corners (mirrored where appropriate so each corner reads as "facing outward")
- Visually distinct from the figures and props in the illustration plane — the corner ornament is on the FRAME plane, not the illustration plane


BANNER TREATMENT FOR CARD NAMES

The bottom banner holding the card name:
- Horizontal rectangular band running across the lower frame area
- Set inside the inner frame line, NOT overlapping the illustration
- Background of the banner: same crisp clean paper as the card body, OR a slightly tinted band (very faint gold-ochre wash) to set the text apart
- Border line of the banner: thin black line consistent with the main frame
- Text style: serif capital letters, black ink, centered
- Text size: readable at deck-card scale (approximately 2.75 x 4.75 inches finished size)

The Roman numeral position (Majors only):
- Centered at the top of the card
- Inside the inner frame
- Could sit inside a small circle, scroll, or simple flourish — or stand alone
- Same gold/ochre tone as corner ornaments for visual unity, OR black to match the frame line
- Note: the artist will replace standard Roman numerals with a West African numeral system in post-production. Generate with placeholder Roman numerals (I, II, III, IV, V, VI, VII, VIII) so the position and size are correct for later substitution.


COVER TITLE PLACEMENT BY OPTION

The cover title "CHILDREN OF MAWU-LISA" placement varies by which cover option is selected:

- Cover Option A (Sketch 1 — split body figure with serpent embrace): Title in horizontal banner at the TOP of the card, above the figure's combined halo. Top-banner placement keeps the central composition (split body + serpent embrace) uncluttered.
- Cover Option B (Sketch 2 — layered Mawu and Lisa): Title in BOTTOM banner. Top space is occupied by the dual halos.
- Cover Option C (Sketch 3 — single figure with split markings): Title in BOTTOM banner. Top space is occupied by the combined halo.
- Cover Option D (Sketch 4 — Aido-Hwedo Ouroboros around earth): Title INSIDE the serpent's embracing curve, below the earth disc, centered. Use the negative space created by the serpent's S-curve as the title's natural home.


BORDER PHYSICAL SPEC (paste into AI VISUALIZATION GUIDANCE)

Use this paragraph as a standard append at the end of every card's AI VISUALIZATION GUIDANCE section so the border renders consistently across the deck:

"The card is framed by a tabletop physical border element constructed from cardstock or thin wood, mounted as the outermost frame plane in front of the puppet stage. The frame is a clean rectangular black line running around the card edge. Modest gold or ochre corner ornaments — small coiled-serpent curl motifs inspired by Dahomey appliqué and Adinkra symbols — sit at all four corners of the frame, mirrored so each curls outward. Inside the frame's bottom edge, a horizontal banner holds the card's name in black serif capital letters (e.g., 'THE HIGH PRIESTESS' for Majors, 'ACE OF CUPS' for Minors). For Major Arcana cards only, a centered Roman numeral sits at the top of the frame inside a small ornamental scroll. The border element is crisp clean paper — NOT aged, weathered, or distressed. The frame reads as a physically built tabletop element, lit by the same practical lamps as the puppet stage."


BORDER PHYSICAL STAGING APPROACH (maquette construction)

How to build the border element if doing physical maquettes:
- Cut a single rectangular frame from heavy cardstock or 1/16" plywood, dimensions proportional to the tarot card aspect ratio (approximately 5:9 width-to-height when scaled to fit the maquette)
- Draw the black frame line in fine-tip black marker or ink along the inner edge of the frame element
- Hand-draw or stamp the corner ornaments in gold or ochre ink at all four corners
- Cut a thin horizontal slot at the bottom of the frame to hold a removable banner card with the card name on it (this lets you reuse the same frame across multiple cards by swapping the banner)
- Cut a small slot at the top of Major Arcana frame elements to hold a removable Roman numeral
- Mount the frame element approximately 1/2 inch in front of the puppet stage on small wooden blocks or museum putty so it sits as the outermost plane
- The frame element should be lit by the same key/fill lamps as the puppet stage, so the lighting feels unified


LAYERED CUTOUT PLANE FOR THE BORDER

When listing layered cutout planes in a card brief, add the border element as the OUTERMOST plane (closest to the camera). Example for a card that previously had four planes:

Before adding border:
- Plane 1: backdrop
- Plane 2: midground silhouette
- Plane 3: puppet
- Plane 4: foreground props

After adding border:
- Plane 1: backdrop
- Plane 2: midground silhouette
- Plane 3: puppet
- Plane 4: foreground props
- Plane 5: BORDER FRAME (outermost, closest to camera, framing the entire scene)


LIGHTING NOTE FOR THE BORDER

The border is a physical element on the maquette stage, so it should be lit by the same lamps as the rest of the scene. However:
- The border itself should NOT cast strong shadows onto the puppet stage behind it — keep the frame element thin enough that it sits flush with the outermost lighting plane
- The gold/ochre corner ornaments should catch the warm side of any split-gel lighting (e.g., on Lovers card with cool/warm split, the gold corners catch the warm side more strongly)
- For cool-dominant scenes (High Priestess, Wheel of Fortune), the gold corners provide a small warm accent that prevents the card from feeling visually one-note


NEGATIVE PROMPTS RELATED TO THE BORDER

Add these to the standard NEGATIVE PROMPTS list when relevant:

"European Art Nouveau botanical border, ornate floral filigree, Victorian decorative frame, gilded ornate gold frame, aged parchment, weathered paper, distress marks, dust spots, vintage stained paper, baroque ornamentation"


REFERENCE PRIORITY NOTE

Use traditional tarot card layouts (Rider-Waite-Smith, Marseille) as a STRUCTURAL reference for border proportions and text placement only — NOT as a stylistic reference. The Children of Mawu-Lisa border departs from European decorative traditions in favor of West African (Dahomey appliqué, Adinkra) motif language. The frame structure is RWS-inspired in cleanness; the ornaments are West African in origin.

END OF BORDER SPECIFICATION
