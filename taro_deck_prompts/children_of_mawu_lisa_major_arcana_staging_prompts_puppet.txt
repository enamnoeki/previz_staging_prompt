CHILDREN OF MAWU-LISA — MAJOR ARCANA STAGING PROMPTS
Cinematic Previsualization Brief — 8 Cards
PUPPET-MODE PREVIZ EDITION


HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

This file contains two layers:

1. SHARED CHASSIS (below) — the deck-wide visual language, staging philosophy, and reusable maquette rigs. Paste this once at the start of any session with an AI image model, previz artist, or art director.

2. 8 CARD PROMPTS — each self-contained, each following the full staging template. Paste any one immediately after the chassis to generate that card.

Reusable batch rigs are noted on each card so you know which physical setup can prototype multiple compositions.

IMPORTANT — TWO DELIVERABLES, ONE BRIEF:
This document describes the PREVIZ STAGE only — tabletop puppet photography used to lock blocking, silhouette, and composition before any illustration work begins. The previz output should look like a visibly handmade puppet on a small physical stage, NOT like a finished illustration or character render. The eventual finished illustration style (Dahomey appliqué, Krobo beadwork, manga face readability) is noted at the bottom of each card under FINAL ILLUSTRATION TARGET, but should NOT influence the previz render.


SHARED CHASSIS — PASTE FIRST

Act as a cinematographer and visual layout artist working on a tarot deck called Children of Mawu-Lisa, rooted in Ewe cosmology. Generate staging, blocking, and composition ideas as cinematic previsualization — tabletop maquette photography using wooden artist's puppets, cloth scraps, paper cutouts, and practical set lighting. The goal is NOT finished illustration. The goal is NOT a character render. The goal is symbolic readability, ceremonial blocking, graphic composition hierarchy, and theatrical emotional staging captured as visibly handmade puppet photography on a small physical stage.

PREVIZ RENDER REQUIREMENT (applies to every card):
The image should read as a photograph of a small tabletop set. Puppets are posable wooden artist's dolls with visible joints, dressed in cloth scraps and paper costume elements. Backdrops are painted paper or fabric. Props are obviously handmade — cardboard, foil, dowel rods, found objects. Lighting is practical lamps with colored gels. The viewer should be able to tell it is a maquette, not an illustration.

STAGING APPROACH:
Treat every scene as a ceremonial tableau / miniature stage play / sacred poster composition / theatrical symbolic freeze-frame — built as a physical tabletop diorama.

PRIORITIES:
Readable silhouettes, emotional clarity, strong focal hierarchy, reusable staging setups, minimal clutter, symbolic object placement, iconic tarot framing, simplified environmental design.

AVOID:
Realism-first rendering, character illustration, anime or manga character renders, 3D character models, stylized human figures, smooth CGI skin, finished illustration, busy backgrounds, decorative clutter, dynamic perspective distortion unless narratively necessary.

REUSABLE MAQUETTE RIGS (build once, reuse across grouped cards):
- RIG A — Throne / Icon Rig: seated wooden puppet on cardboard throne block, frontal symmetrical staging, top-down or side-key lighting, hanging halo cutout overhead. Prototypes High Priestess, Empress, Emperor (and Queen of Swords/Pentacles when you build them).
- RIG B — Duality / Mirror Rig: two wooden puppets facing each other on centerline, split-gel lighting (cool/warm), reflective acrylic floor or mirror panel between them. Prototypes The Lovers (and Two of Cups).
- RIG C — Cosmic Emblem Rig: no figure. Circular paper cutout or coiled rope on dark cloth, overhead light rig, radial composition. Prototypes Wheel of Fortune (and Card Back).
- RIG D — Transformation / Power Rig: wooden puppet with raffia/cloth silhouette extensions, low side key with red or amber gel, textured paper ground, optional torn-paper smoke and flashlight lightning streaks. Prototypes Death, Tower, Sun, Knight of Wands.

THUMBNAIL WORKFLOW RECOMMENDATION:
Before any AI generation or final render, produce 3 value thumbnails per card (2x3 inch, grayscale, 5-minute limit each) testing silhouette clarity at deck-size (approximately 2.75 x 4.75 in). If the silhouette doesn't read at thumbnail scale, the staging fails — revise blocking before adding any detail.


CARD 1 — THE HIGH PRIESTESS (MAWU)
Rig: A — Throne / Icon

SCENE PURPOSE:
Embodied stillness and hidden knowledge. The viewer should feel watched by something that already knows the answer.

ENVIRONMENT:
Minimal moon chamber — deep indigo void with a quiet scatter of stars or a single moon-phase frieze behind her. No architecture. Negative space dominates.

CAMERA:
Low frontal medium shot, eye-level to her chest. Slight upward tilt to emphasize her stillness as monumental. Long-lens compression — flatten the depth so she reads as iconic, not spatial.

CHARACTER ACTION:
Seated wooden puppet, still. Wooden hands resting on lap or cradling a small calabash prop. Wooden head turned directly toward lens.

FOCAL POINT:
The puppet's head, then the crescent moon cutout floating directly above. Eye-to-moon vertical axis is the entire composition.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Centered vertical composition. Puppet occupies the lower two-thirds, halo cutout and negative space the upper third. Small serpent prop (coiled cord or pipe-cleaner) at her feet or wrapped around her forearm — small, low in frame.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Calabash prop held at lap level on centerline. Moon halo cutout floats directly above the puppet's wooden head with a thin gap of negative space — do not let them touch. Serpent prop silhouette breaks the lower contour of her cloth robe to create a readable shape at the base.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Strict bilateral symmetry. Layered cloth scraps draped over the puppet instead of fabric detail. Triangle silhouette anchored by her seated mass, capped by the moon disc cutout. Heavy negative space above and to the sides.

LIGHTING:
Top-down moonlight. Cool blue-white key from directly overhead, falling on the puppet's wooden crown, shoulders, and the upper curve of the calabash prop. Face in soft fill — readable but quiet. No warm tones anywhere.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:
- Calabash prop (lap, centerline) — small clay bowl or half a wooden ball
- Serpent prop (feet or forearm) — coiled cord, pipe cleaner, or twisted wire
- Crescent moon cutout (above head) — white foamcore or cardstock
- Moon-phase markings dotted on the puppet's wooden face in white paint or chalk

MOOD / INFLUENCE:
Quiet omniscience. Editorial illustration clarity meets ceremonial portraiture — translated into puppet photography.

READABILITY GOAL:
The audience must feel watched and known. Silhouette must read at thumbnail size as: seated triangular figure plus disc above head.

PERSPECTIVE GRID:
1-point perspective with vanishing point dead-centered behind her head. Horizon centered. Vanishing point hidden behind figure — no environmental lines competing.

CHARACTER ORIENTATION:
Front view. Wooden torso facing camera. Head straight, eyes to lens. Symmetrical.

PHYSICAL STAGING APPROACH (maquette):
- Seated wooden artist's puppet on a low cardboard throne block
- Hanging moon cutout (white foamcore disc, suspended on fishing line from above)
- Blue/cool gel over a single overhead lamp
- Reflective foil scraps on the calabash prop for moonlight highlights
- Dark indigo cloth backdrop, no creases visible

LAYERED CUTOUT IDEAS:
Three depth planes — (1) backdrop indigo, (2) seated puppet silhouette, (3) calabash and serpent props in foreground. Cut moon halo as a separate plane suspended between (1) and (2).

ACRYLIC GRID SUGGESTION:
Overlay a thirds grid with a centered vertical axis. Puppet's eye line should fall on upper-third horizontal; calabash on lower-third horizontal; moon halo above the upper-third line.

COLORED GEL LIGHTING:
Single CTB (cool blue) gel on overhead key. Optional very faint lavender rim from behind to separate her shoulders from the indigo backdrop.

CARD 2 — THE EMPRESS (NANA BULUKU)
Rig: A — Throne / Icon

SCENE PURPOSE:
Maternal abundance. Ancient, unhurried fertility. The viewer should feel held.

ENVIRONMENT:
Symbolic garden-throne against a deep earth-tone field — ochre, terracotta, or forest green. A few stylized vine and palm silhouettes frame her, but no full environment. The throne IS the garden.

CAMERA:
Straight-on medium-wide. Eye level or very slightly low. No tilt. Stable, patient framing — the camera, like her, does not move.

CHARACTER ACTION:
Seated wooden puppet. Heavy, relaxed posture — one wooden hand resting on her cloth-wrapped belly or knee, the other resting on the throne or holding a small plant prop.

FOCAL POINT:
The puppet's grounded body mass, then the vegetation silhouette wreathing her. Centered triangle reading as earth plus growth.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Large grounded triangular silhouette filling the lower two-thirds of frame. Fake vines, palm fronds, and yam-shaped props extending from the throne edges into the midground. Small calabash prop beside her foot.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Plant or fruit prop held at chest/belly height. Calabash low-left or low-right at ground level. Cloth gele crown frames the top of the silhouette — don't let foliage compete with it for the top focal zone.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Strong stable triangle. Horizontal weight at the base. Layered cloth scraps (her wrappers) read as nested shapes. Pattern density on draped fabric contrasts with quiet background.

LIGHTING:
Warm side glow from camera-right, low angle. Soft fill from camera-left. Top stays slightly darker — she emerges from warm earth shadow rather than being spotlit.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:
- Fake vines, palm fronds, yam-shaped props woven into throne
- Small plant or fruit prop in one hand
- Calabash prop at her feet
- Cloth gele crown wrapped around the puppet's wooden head, decorated with small cowrie shells and paper leaves
- Fine parallel lines painted on the puppet's wooden cheeks (ancestral age marks)

MOOD / INFLUENCE:
Ancient fertility. Ceremonial portrait energy — matriarch in full regalia — translated into puppet photography.

READABILITY GOAL:
Audience must feel her as the source. Silhouette reads at thumbnail as: heavy grounded triangle with a crowned dome on top.

PERSPECTIVE GRID:
1-point perspective, low horizon emphasizing her scale. Vanishing point behind her at chest level.

CHARACTER ORIENTATION:
Front view. Wooden torso facing camera. Slight settling weight to one hip — readable but not posed.

- Layered cloth scraps (felt or fabric remnants) for wrapper masses

- Low warm tungsten lamp camera-right

LAYERED CUTOUT IDEAS:
Four planes — (1) earth-tone backdrop, (2) far foliage silhouette layer, (3) puppet plus throne, (4) foreground calabash and dropped paper leaves. Foliage planes 2 and 4 should echo each other in shape to frame her.

ACRYLIC GRID SUGGESTION:
Thirds grid with a centered vertical. Belly/hand on center crosshair. Gele crown on upper-third horizontal. Calabash on lower-third intersection.

COLORED GEL LIGHTING:
Warm amber (CTO or 1/2 CTO) on key. Optional deep green gel on a small backlight to push a single foliage edge for separation.

CARD 3 — THE EMPEROR (LISA)
Rig: A — Throne / Icon

SCENE PURPOSE:
Authority through structure. Order, daylight, the principle of work and shape.

ENVIRONMENT:
Abstract forge-throne — warm ochre, deep red, or burnt orange field with a quiet sunburst pattern radiating behind him. A small anvil silhouette at his feet grounds the forge association without building a full smithy.

CAMERA:
Slight low-angle hero framing, medium shot. Camera at the puppet's sternum level looking slightly up. Lens compression keeps the sun disc tight to his crown.

CHARACTER ACTION:
Wooden puppet seated or standing — frontal, upright, rigid. Dowel-rod iron staff held vertically in one wooden hand.

FOCAL POINT:
Sun disc cutout behind his head, then the staff. Vertical axis: staff to torso to sun disc.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Tall vertical dominance. Puppet occupies a narrow central column. Paper sun rays radiate outward from behind him into the upper two-thirds. Small painted anvil block low-foreground, slightly offset from centerline.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Dowel staff held vertically, parallel to frame edge — do not angle it. Sun disc cutout directly behind head, partially eclipsed by his wooden crown. Anvil prop at his feet, slight 3/4 angle so its silhouette reads.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Vertical rectangle silhouette. Radial paper sunburst behind creates a halo of repeating lines — keep them stylized and graphic, not realistic rays. Hard edges throughout.

LIGHTING:
Hard warm side lighting from camera-left, slightly raised. Strong contrast. Deep shadow on camera-right side of figure to carve the silhouette.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:

PERSPECTIVE GRID:

CHARACTER ORIENTATION:

CARD 4 — THE LOVERS (MAWU-LISA)
Rig: B — Duality / Mirror

SCENE PURPOSE:
Union through balance. Divine duality made intimate. Not romance — completion.

ENVIRONMENT:
Minimal cosmic gradient backdrop. Split or vertical gradient: indigo (Mawu's side, screen-left) transitioning to ochre (Lisa's side, screen-right). Optional sky band showing moon upper-left and sun upper-right.

CAMERA:
Wide symmetrical frontal shot. Eye level. Centered on the meeting point between the two puppets. No tilt.

CHARACTER ACTION:
Two wooden puppets facing each other in profile. Wooden foreheads touching, or wooden hands joined at the centerline. Mirrored posture — whatever Mawu does, Lisa does in reverse.

FOCAL POINT:
The connection point — touching wooden foreheads or joined wooden hands — dead center frame.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Mirror composition. Mawu puppet screen-left in profile facing right. Lisa puppet screen-right in profile facing left. Centerline is the entire compositional spine. Coiled cord serpent at their feet, uniting them.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Joined wooden hands or touching wooden foreheads on exact centerline. If hands are joined, they hold either calabash prop (Mawu's side) or iron tool prop (Lisa's side) — or both meeting between them. Sun cutout upper-right corner, moon cutout upper-left corner if used.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Strict bilateral mirror. Every shape on Mawu's side has a corresponding shape on Lisa's. Cloth-scrap garments echo each other in pattern logic but invert in palette. Ceremonial wedding tableau energy.

LIGHTING:
Split warm/cool dual lighting. Cool blue key from screen-left on Mawu, warm amber key from screen-right on Lisa. Lights cross at the centerline where they meet — the meeting point is where both lights overlap, creating a soft neutral pool.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:
- Joined wooden hands at center
- Coiled cord serpent at their feet, uniting them
- Moon cutout (upper-left) and sun cutout (upper-right) — optional
- Mirrored headpieces: Mawu's crescent moon cutout, Lisa's sun disc cutout, meeting at the centerline

MOOD / INFLUENCE:
Sacred intimacy. Theatrical ceremonial — wedding rite, not lovers' embrace — translated into puppet photography.

READABILITY GOAL:
Silhouette must read at thumbnail as: two profiles meeting at center plus dual halo shapes (crescent plus disc) at top.

PERSPECTIVE GRID:
Flat staging — no perspective grid needed. Centered horizon, vanishing points pushed off-frame to eliminate depth distortion. Profile silhouettes should read as pure shape.

CHARACTER ORIENTATION:
Both wooden puppets in side/profile view. Mawu screen-left facing right; Lisa screen-right facing left. Wooden heads slightly tilted so foreheads can meet without overlapping nose silhouettes. Bodies square to centerline.

PHYSICAL STAGING APPROACH (maquette):
- Two wooden artist's puppets on centerline
- Reflective acrylic or mirror panel between them on the floor to suggest reflection
- Split-color gel rig: blue camera-left, amber camera-right
- Center spotlight or soft top light on the meeting point
- Gradient backdrop painted or printed (indigo-to-ochre vertical split)

LAYERED CUTOUT IDEAS:
Five planes — (1) gradient backdrop, (2) moon cutout upper-left, (3) sun cutout upper-right, (4) Mawu puppet, (5) Lisa puppet. Coiled cord serpent as a separate low foreground plane.

ACRYLIC GRID SUGGESTION:
Strict centerline axis. Profile noses on the vertical thirds lines (Mawu's nose on left-third, Lisa's nose on right-third) so faces don't crowd the meeting point.

COLORED GEL LIGHTING:
CTB blue gel screen-left key. CTO amber gel screen-right key. Lights set to equal intensity — neither puppet should dominate. Optional faint magenta or lavender backlight on the meeting point to mark it as sacred.

CARD 5 — WHEEL OF FORTUNE (AIDO-HWEDO)
Rig: C — Cosmic Emblem

SCENE PURPOSE:
The universe turning endlessly. Cosmic order. The only card without a human figure — which makes it land harder.

ENVIRONMENT:
Deep cosmic blue or black void. Optional scattered stars, kept sparse and graphic — dots, not realistic starfield.

CAMERA:
Perfect frontal. Camera dead-on, perpendicular to the wheel. No tilt, no angle. The serpent must read as a flat emblem.

CHARACTER ACTION:
None. The serpent prop is the action — coiled into a great circle, biting its own tail.

FOCAL POINT:
Tail-mouth connection. Where the bite happens — this point can glow or be marked.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Circular enclosure dominates the frame — serpent fills 70-80% of vertical space, centered. Earth-disc or four elemental Trowo symbol cutouts inside the ring at N/S/E/W positions.

OBJECT POSITIONING:
Mouth-tail bite point at top center, OR at the 3 o'clock position — pick one and commit. Four elemental symbol cutouts (water, fire, wind, earth) inside the ring at compass points, small and equal in weight.

COMPOSITION NOTES:
Pure radial composition. Emblem-driven. The serpent prop's surface should be two-toned — one half cool (Mawu side), one half warm (Lisa side) — painted onto the rope or sculpted form. Bilateral or radial symmetry.

LIGHTING:
Radial cosmic glow. Soft light emanating from the center outward, OR a glow from behind the serpent ring giving it a halo. No directional key — this is symbolic light, not staged light.

SYMBOLIC OBJECTS:
- Coiled rope or thick cord serpent prop in a circle, biting its tail
- Four small paper elemental symbol cutouts at compass points inside the ring
- Optional stylized earth-disc cutout at center
- Mouth-tail bite point as the focal mark

MOOD / INFLUENCE:
Timeless inevitability. Sacred poster. The most graphic, least cinematic card — closer to a sigil than a scene.

READABILITY GOAL:
Silhouette must read at thumbnail as: a single circle. That's the test. If it doesn't read as a clean ring at deck size, it fails.

PERSPECTIVE GRID:
No perspective. Pure flat radial. Centered, no horizon, no vanishing points. This card is iconographic — perspective would weaken it.

ORIENTATION:
Frontal flat emblem. No 3D rotation of the ring. Serpent body wraps in a plane parallel to the camera.

PHYSICAL STAGING APPROACH (maquette):
- Coiled rope or thick painted cord on a dark cloth, arranged into a clean circle
- Circular paper cutouts for the elemental symbols, placed at N/S/E/W inside the ring
- Overhead single light rig with a soft diffuser
- Optional small LED inside the ring at the bite point for glow

LAYERED CUTOUT IDEAS:
Three planes only — (1) deep void backdrop with painted star dots, (2) serpent rope ring, (3) inner elemental symbol cutouts. Keep flat. Resist the urge to add depth.

ACRYLIC GRID SUGGESTION:
Concentric circle grid centered on frame. Outer ring equals serpent rope. Inner ring equals elemental positions. Center equals optional earth-disc. Test that the bite point sits cleanly on a cardinal axis.

COLORED GEL LIGHTING:
Split the ring lighting: cool blue gel on the Mawu half of the rope (screen-left), warm amber on the Lisa half (screen-right). Lights meet at top and bottom of the ring. Background stays unlit cosmic blue.

AI VISUALIZATION GUIDANCE:
Tabletop maquette photography. Visibly handmade overhead diorama. Thick painted rope coiled into a perfect circle on a dark cloth backdrop, arranged so one end appears to bite the other (ouroboros). Rope painted two-toned: one half cool indigo, one half warm ochre. Four small paper elemental symbol cutouts placed at compass points inside the ring. Painted star dots on the dark cloth. Overhead diffused lighting with split gels — blue on the indigo half, amber on the ochre half. Pure frontal radial composition, no perspective, no figures. Render as overhead set photography, not as illustration.

NEGATIVE PROMPTS:
realistic snake, 3D rendering, anime, character illustration, finished illustration, dynamic angle, cluttered iconography, human figures.

CARD 6 — DEATH (SAKPATA)
Rig: D — Transformation / Power

SCENE PURPOSE:
Transformation without horror. The keeper of the earth's ledger — dignified, inevitable, neither menacing nor mournful.

ENVIRONMENT:
Abstract cracked-earth stage. Deep red earth or burnt sienna ground, possibly with a cracked-ground texture rendered as graphic shapes drawn in marker on textured paper. Backdrop: a darker red-brown painted-paper field. No architecture.

CAMERA:
Low medium shot. Camera at the puppet's knee level looking up slightly. Stable, no tilt. He should feel monumental but not aggressive.

CHARACTER ACTION:
Wooden puppet standing frontal or three-quarter. Still but imposing. One wooden hand raised — in benediction or warning, ambiguous on purpose.

FOCAL POINT:
The raised wooden hand, then the cowrie-topped staff. The raised hand is the gesture that defines the card.

SPATIAL BLOCKING:
Heavy grounded silhouette filling center frame. Dowel staff held vertically or slightly angled in one wooden hand. Cracked ground reads as graphic marker lines at the base.
